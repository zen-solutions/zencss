Getting Started

Kick things off by downloading or cloning the main branch, then navigate to docs -> examples -> hello-world.html. Once it's up and running, you’re just a container and row declaration away from wrapping your HTML content in ZenCSS goodness.

Dive into a world where color and form come together seamlessly, and everything just falls into place to keep the vibes balanced. And don’t sweat it if you’re already rocking Bootstrap—ZenCSS plays nice and makes it a breeze to get them working together.

Quick Overview - The zenCSS "Smart Align" Framework

z-container

Containers are individual sections on your webpage, like this blue one right here. They're all about keeping things tidy and aligned, no matter if someone’s checking out your site on a mobile, tablet, or desktop.

Dive into the details below, and feel free to come back to tweak this container—add or remove some columns or rows. We think you'll find our setup simple, effective, and classy.

z-row

Think of rows as the building blocks within a container, stackable like layers on a cake. Add as many or as few as you need—the smart flex logic in ZenCSS makes sure they play nice together.

Rows hold any number of columns (three in this case), which in turn are the place for your HTML elements to live. Since this is a quick start we won't expand on the nesting logic.

z-col

Columns are where the magic happens. Whether they’re showcasing an image with a section heading and text, or a themed layout inspired by the Fibonacci sequence, they’re up for it.

Columns auto-adjust width based on their neighbors and align flawlessly on both tablet and mobile views, even if in different rows Here, they act as themed cards, marrying form and function.

 ✓   Right click to inspect this element, then select -> right click z-rows/z-columns and duplicate or delete to see our "smart align technology" at work.

Kickstarting with a Basic Example

Let's begin with a straightforward example of defining a container, row and column using semantic HTML: 

<main class="z-container">
  <section class="z-row">
    <div class="z-col"> ... </div>
  </section>
</main>

The beauty of ZenCSS is in its go-with-the-flow nature. Whether you're packing a row with 2 or 6 columns, or filling a column with waaay too many things, ZenCSS handles the layout with grace. Want to jazz up a content area? Just toss in more rows or columns. Yep, it’s that straightforward.

If you're all about keeping things snappy, say hello to Zentax—our sleek shorthand HTML syntax that'll slash your coding effort in half: 

<z-container>
  <z-row>
    <z-col> ... </z-col>
  </z-row>
</z-container>

The zenCSS Color System - Accessible Color Themes for Captivating UI Designs

Step into a palette of possibilities with 6 unique themes and a myriad of classes dedicated to rendering various hues and styles for your content. The zenCSS Color System is your ally in creating visually captivating layouts with diverse degrees of contrast.

Automatically adhering to the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines with a color contrast ratio of 4.5:1*, zenCSS simplifies the journey of keeping your UI not only stylish but also accessible, blending aesthetics with inclusivity seamlessly.

*in light mode (dark mode has alpha support)

zenCSS Text System - Automatic, dynamic, responsive resizing

zenCSS comes with a dynamic text scaling feature that takes the guesswork out of font sizing across varying viewport resolutions, ensuring readability and aesthetic consistency are always in check.

Right out of the gate, our standard text sizes are set as follows:

  • 14px on mobile
  • 16 px on tablet
  • 18 on desktop screens

The heading tags (h1 - h6) are cleverly set up to resize at each breakpoint, based on the default font size. For a closer look, inspect an element below and switch to the computed tab in your browser's developer tools while toggling the responsive view. As you resize the window, you'll see the font sizes gracefully adapting to the changing viewport dimensions.

Controlling how content breaks

zenCSS is all about ensuring a graceful display and realignment of items across devices. Yet, if you fancy some extra control, our utility classes are here to let you fine-tune the layout. By default, zenCSS rolls out four columns on desktop, two on tablet, and one on mobile. For example, with six children in a row, tossing in the class two-up to the row will have each child cozying up to half the width. Similarly, throw in three-up and each child will claim a third of the space.

Experimental feature

Debugging Stylistic Issues chrome extension available

Navigating through CSS debugging can get knotty with mobile/tablet variations, pre-processors, absent source maps, and minification in the mix.

Enter "Zen Test" in zenCSS—a handy tool that overlays outlines on content, making bug identification and resolution a lot more intuitive. It pairs well with your browser's inspect element tool, simplifying the task of isolating issues.

Mobile troubleshooting becomes a breeze when you can quickly pinpoint where bugs are cropping up.

Outlines draw the element boundaries with blue dotted lines for containers (purple for -fluid), rows (green), and columns (red), while paragraphs and headings sport varying shades of orange outlines. An alignment grid is brewing on our end.

Manual Installation

Get zenCSS up and running on your site by linking to our CSS file in the HTML head section, and including the zencss.js file just before the closing body tag. 

<link rel="stylesheet" href="path-to/zen.css">
<script src="path-to/zencss.js"></script>

Usage

Deploy zenCSS in two ways: globally across the site, or selectively on specific HTML elements.

Globally: Declare zen on your body to apply zenCSS site-wide. 

<body class="zen"> 
...
</body>

Mix in: Invoke zen on any HTML element to harness zenCSS's power locally, without disrupting existing elements. 

<section class="zen"> 
...
</section>
While working inside a parent element declared as zen, you're what we refer to as "in zen", granting access to all zenCSS tools and features. Return to where you were when you followed the simple setup steps.
