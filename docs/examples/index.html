    Meet zenCSS

    An open-source website authoring and design toolkit that helps you build, style, and debug websites. Whether harnessing your mastery of a code editor or just beginning your HTML and CSS journey, you're about to build beautiful solutions with our tools. Let's dive in.

    What's included?

    Framework/Design System

    Ensure your content displays beautifully. 6 UI skins, automatic formatting of content/ text, testing/debugging toolkit, and dark mode.

    Utility Toolkit

    Over 100 utility classes that allow you to tweak, shape, test, debug and beautify your core design. Customize type, color and form to your heart's content.

    UI content library

    A collection of off-the-shelf UI components you use immediately to build websites better and faster. Soon, a marketplace featuring top designers.

    Getting Started

    Begin by following the straightforward setup instructions provided here to enable our toolkit on your site. Once activated, working with our tool is as simple as declaring a container and row to encapsulate your HTML content. Experience a seamless blend of color and form as everything self-adjusts to maintain a balanced aesthetic. Rest easy deploying zenCSS alongside established frameworks like Bootstrap, enhancing your design without conflict.

    Desktop-First Design Philosophy

    Embrace a desktop-centric workflow. Craft your website's content for desktop interfaces, and trust zenCSS to adeptly translate the design for tablet and mobile presentations, ensuring a harmonious user experience across all device landscapes.

    Quick Overview - The zenCSS Grid System

    • Containers - Consider containers as distinct sections of your webpage, akin to cake layers. Each container is a resolution-friendly box, adept at managing horizontal alignment across mobile, tablet, and desktop devices. For a full-width container, employ the container-fluid class.
    • Rows - Rows stretch to fill 100% width of their parent container, housing columns. They come sans padding or margin, with smart flex logic embedded to ensure they "just work." Alter the default behavior to your liking using our custom classes.
    • Columns - Columns are the cradles for your HTML elements. Whether it's a heading, a paragraph paired with an image, or a themed design, columns are up for the task. They come with margin and padding, aligning to the top-left by default, and auto-adjust for tablet and mobile views.

    Kickstarting with a Basic Example

    Let's begin with a straightforward example of our signature Grid layout for a site section using semantic HTML: 

    <main class="z-container">
  <section class="z-row">
    <div class="z-col"> ... </div>
  </section>
</main>

    The flexibility of zenCSS shines through as the number of columns per row (typically 2-6) or HTML items per column is irrelevant—zenCSS manages the layout gracefully. Enrich a content area by adding more rows or columns. It’s that simple.

    If brevity appeals to you, meet Zentax, our shorthand HTML syntax that halves your coding effort: 

    <z-container>
  <z-row>
    <z-col> ... </z-col>
  </z-row>
</z-container>

    Dynamic, responsive text resizing

    zenCSS embodies a dynamic text scaling feature, auto-adjusting font sizes across varying viewport resolutions to ensure readability and aesthetic consistency.

    Out of the box, our standard text sizes are:

    • 14px on mobile
    • 16 px on tablet
    • 18 on desktop screens

    The heading tags (h1 - h6) are smartly configured to resize at each breakpoint, based on the default font size. For a deeper dive, inspect an element below and switch to the computed tab in your browser's developer tools while toggling the responsive view. As you resize the window, you'll observe the font sizes adapting to the changing viewport dimensions.

    Adjusting text scaling to meet your preferences is straightforward with zenCSS. Overriding the font-size in a specific media query just once triggers a cascade effect, updating the sizes of all font elements accordingly.

    Controlling how content breaks

    zenCSS aims for a graceful display and realignment of items across devices. However, if desired, you can leverage our utility classes to fine-tune the layout. By default, zenCSS shows three columns on desktop, two on tablet, and one on mobile. For instance, with six children in a row, adding the class two-up to the row makes each child occupy 50% of the width. Similarly, use four-up and each child will take a quarter of the space.

    Debugging Stylistic Issues

    Debugging CSS can be tricky, especially with mobile/tablet variations, pre-processors, lack of source maps, and minification.

    The "Zen Test" in zenCSS to overlay outlines on content, aiding in bug identification and resolution. This pairs well with your browser's inspect element tool, making issue isolation swift. Outlines indicate container boundaries (purple for fluid containers), rows (blue), and columns (red), while paragraphs get a thin orange outline. An alignment grid is on the horizon.     Installation

    Get zenCSS up and running on your site by linking to our CSS file in the HTML head section, and including the zencss.js file just before the closing body tag. 

    <link rel="stylesheet" href="path-to/zen.css">
    <script src="path-to/zencss.js"></script>

    Usage

    Deploy zenCSS in two ways: globally across the site, or selectively on specific HTML elements.

    Globally: Declare zen on your body to apply zenCSS site-wide. 

    <body class="zen"> 
...
</body>

    Mix in: Invoke zen on any HTML element to harness zenCSS's power locally, without disrupting existing elements. 

    <section class="zen"> 
...
</section>
    While coding within a parent tagged as zen, you're "in zen", granting access to all zenCSS tools and features. Return to where you were when you followed the simple setup steps.
