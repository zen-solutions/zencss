What is the ZEN ideology?

The ZEN (Zero Effort Notation) ideology presents a unified approach to styling web applications, reimagining the best practices from BEM, utility-first CSS, and atomic design.

Why practice ZEN?

ZEN's structured yet adaptable process simplifies UI development while ensuring consistency across the design. This system is designed for modularity, scalability, and cross-team clarity. The "form before function" strategy facilitates easy design modification without overhauling foundational styles. Intuitive nomenclature reduces maintenance effort.

ZEN is ideal for both individual developers and teams, streamlining workflows and promoting efficiency and predictability. Adopt ZEN for a perfect blend of structure and flexibility in your web projects.

Core Principles

We Use a Hierarchical Structure:

  • Elements: Basic building blocks such as images and buttons.
  • Components: Items like a card, modal or z-col that encapsulate a series of elements.
  • Sections: Groupings of components to form parts of a webpage.

Each unit above is individually themable and customizable for consistent yet flexible styling.

Example: A headline and text (elements), inside a z-col card (components), making up the right portion of a page content area (section).

We Recognise Style and Functional Modifiers:

6 distinct types of classes that define the appearance and behavior of your website content.

Order of application: Type → State → Theme → Background → Text → Style

<z-col class="card disabled theme-dark bg-white text-black italic">
    <!-- Your content goes here -->
</z-col>
Note: you'd rarely use all types of modifiers on the same piece of content. This example is for illustration purposes only.

We Support State Management (WIP):

Control the display and visibility of elements through state classes.

States include: Active, disabled, Hidden, Visible, and display types like Block, Inline-block, etc.

We Use A Common Structural Layout:

Organize layouts with a clear structural hierarchy.

  • Elements/Components: Within Columns for organization.
  • Columns: Within Rows for horizontal flow.
  • Rows/Containers: Stack to form vertical sections of a page.
  • Container-fluid: For full-width sections or vertical spacing.

We auto-optimize across all devices - but you have the final say.

Leverage AI in your optimization process.

Each component is auto-optimized for desktop, tablet and mobile display. Every site section, collection of parts, and elements themselves. Everything is modifiable by the end user.
