The zenCSS Smart-Align Framework

Dive into a world where shape and form align, and everything magically falls into place.

Our journey begins with a container.

Imagine containers as the foundational blocks of your webpage, holding your content with precision and care. For example, this grey section is a container, a self-contained universe of your design.

Their mission? To ensure your layouts stay sharp and consistent, adapting seamlessly to the screens of mobiles, tablets, and desktops alike. We use the "z-" prefix in our framework structure, so this is referred to as a z-container.

Rows (z-row) are declared within containers, and are akin to layers in a delicious cake - each adding its own flavor to the overall experience.

Stack as many or as few as you desire — our Smart-Align Framework is the master chef, ensuring rows align perfectly, creating a visual feast.


And inside rows... columns (z-col). Any amount is fine, columns format automatically Or use a container to make a micro-layout in a given section 1 2 3

By default, the Smart-Align System stacks four columns on desktop, two on tablet and one on mobile. Resize the window to see it in action.

1 2 3 4

You may use "x-up" prefix to specify modifier classes that change the default alignment (where x is a number from two through eight). here we add the class eight-up to the row.

And you can nest containers in columns if you need more items per row. Everything will automatically align, beautifully.

Code Example

<div class="z-container">
  <div class="z-row">
    <div class="z-col">
    ...
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

Or use Zentax and write less code

<z-container>
    <z-row>
      <z-col>
      ...
      </z-col>
    </z-row>
</z-container>
