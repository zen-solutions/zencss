The zenCSS Smart-Align Framework

Dive into a world where shape and form align, and everything magically falls into place.

Your journey begins with a container.

Imagine containers as the foundational blocks of your webpage, holding your content with precision and care. For example, this grey section is a container.

Their mission? To ensure your layouts stay sharp and adapt seamlessly to the screens of mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.


Rows are declared within containers, and are akin to layers in a delicious cake - each adding its own flavor to the overall experience.

Stack as many or as few as you desire — our Smart-Align Framework is the master chef, ensuring rows align perfectly, creating a visual feast.


And inside rows. columns. Any amount is fine, columns also align automatically Or nest a container to make a micro-layout in a given section 1 2 3

By default, the Smart-Align System stacks four columns on desktop, two on tablet and one on mobile. You can modify this behavior, but the system is dynamic and will adjust to any amount of columns automatically. Odd columns size up accordingly. Resize the window to see it in action.

1 2 3 4 5 6 7

You may use "x-up" prefix to specify modifier classes that change the default alignment (where x is a number from two through eight). here we add the class eight-up to the row. We also target mobile with the .mobile-two-up class so devices smaller than tablet will display columns side by side.

1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8

One more thing.. If you want to go full screen, you'd use container-fluid. Note: We use a "z-" prefix for container, row and column class names.

HTML Example

<div class="z-container">
  <div class="z-row">
    <div class="z-col">
    ...
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

Zentax Example

<z-container>
    <z-row>
      <z-col>
      ...
      </z-col>
    </z-row>
</z-container>
